Our Mission

Brukner Nature Center is a non-profit privately funded organization promoting the appreciation and understanding of wildlife conservation through preservation, education, and rehabilitation. Nonmembers may use the “Donate” button or the Drop-box by our front door to pay for their admission for the trails. Sorry, no pets allowed… they scare our wildlife!

Membership

Your membership supports our mission to promote the appreciation and understanding of wildlife conservation through preservation, education, and rehabilitation. Benefits of membership include:

  • Free admission seven days a week
  • Our bi-monthly newsletter – The Gray Fox Gazette
  • Reduced fees on programs
  • Reciprocal admission to over 170 other nature centers across the country
  • a 10% discount in our Nature Shop
  • Full use of our Nature Library!

    River's Edge Wildlife Preserve

    Visit our new property located just a few miles southwest of the nature center. The River’s Edge Wildlife Preserve currently features one trail known as the Cascade Loop. This roughly mile-long trail follows along the top of a ravine that straddles the Stillwater River, providing excellent opportunities for birdwatching in the treetops below! It then passes a small limestone cascade that flows in the wetter months. The trail provides access to a unique ecosystem supporting rare flora and fauna. The trail follows an old access road down into the forested valley then back up a gently sloping rise as it returns to the parking lot.
    Directions to River's Edge Wildlife Preserve (from our main property) Turn left out of Brukner Nature Center onto Horseshoe Bend Road. Take the first right onto South Greenlee Road. Turn right on State Route 55 where you will pass over the Stillwater River. Immediately after crossing the bridge, turn left on Calumet Road. The parking area will be on your left and consists of a gravel lot with space for 10 cars. From the parking area, access the paver walkway leading to an information panel and our first and only trail, the Cascades Loop.

    River’s Edge Wildlife Preserve is open daily from sunrise to sunset and is free to visit. There are no restrooms available on-site and no pets are allowed.
